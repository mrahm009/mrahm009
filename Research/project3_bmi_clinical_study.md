# Project 3: Comprehensive Arterial Health Assessment Across BMI Groups

## Overview

Designed and executed a multi-visit IRB-approved clinical study measuring arterial pulse signals in healthy male subjects across varying BMI ranges, evaluating sensor performance against established clinical reference measurements.

---

## Motivation

The performance of wearable cardiovascular sensors varies significantly with body composition, particularly subcutaneous tissue thickness. This study investigated how BMI affects arterial pulse signal quality and the reliability of derived physiological parameters, with direct implications for the clinical utility of wearable sensing devices across diverse patient populations.

---

## Study Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **IRB Approval** | #1452174-27 |
| **Population** | Healthy male subjects |
| **BMI Groups** | <25 kg/m² and 30–40 kg/m² |
| **Sensor Designs** | Two microfluidic tactile sensor designs (varying PDMS thickness) |
| **Measurement Sites** | Radial and carotid arteries |
| **Visit Structure** | Multi-visit longitudinal protocol |

---

## Data Collection Protocol

### Wearable Sensor Measurements
- Microfluidic tactile sensor at radial and carotid arteries
- Data collected at rest and across **six post-exercise time points**:
  - Immediately post-exercise
  - 10, 15, 20, 30, and 60 minutes post-exercise

### Reference Clinical Measurements
- **Doppler ultrasound**: arterial diameter, blood flow velocity, volumetric flow rate
- **Applanation tonometry**: vascular stiffness via ECG-gated tonometer
- **Brachial blood pressure**: standard cuff measurement

### Derived Physiological Parameters
- Heart rate
- Respiration rate
- Augmentation index
- Pulse wave velocity (PWV)
